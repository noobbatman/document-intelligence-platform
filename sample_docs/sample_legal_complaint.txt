                        PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

1. Plaintiffs Margaret A. Cole and Thomas B. Cole bring this action against Defendant
Midwest Fidelity Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") for unlawfully disclosing their private financial
records to federal investigators without lawful authority and without notice, in violation of
the Right to Financial Privacy Act, 12 U.S.C. § 3401 et seq., and for the Bank's negligent
handling of a litigation hold placed on Plaintiffs' accounts.

                        JURISDICTION AND VENUE

2. This Court has jurisdiction over this action pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1331, as this
action arises under the laws of the United States.

3. This Court also has jurisdiction pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1343 for claims arising
under federal civil rights statutes.

4. Venue is proper in this district pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b) because a substantial
part of the events or omissions giving rise to the claims occurred in this judicial district,
and the Defendant maintains branches and conducts substantial business in this district.

                        PARTIES

5. Plaintiff Margaret A. Cole is an individual residing in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin.

6. Plaintiff Thomas B. Cole is an individual residing in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin.

7. Defendant Midwest Fidelity Bank, N.A. is a national banking association organized
under the laws of the United States, with its principal place of business at 250 West Water
Street, Milwaukee, Wisconsin 53202. The Bank is a "financial institution" as defined in
12 U.S.C. § 3401(1).

                        FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

8. Plaintiffs have maintained personal checking, savings, and investment accounts with
the Bank since 2009.

9. In March 2023, federal investigators from the Department of Justice contacted the Bank
and requested records pertaining to Plaintiffs' accounts covering the period of January 2020
through December 2022.

10. On or about April 4, 2023, without providing Plaintiffs with notice, challenge rights,
or any opportunity to object as required by 12 U.S.C. §§ 3405-3407, the Bank disclosed
account statements, wire transfer records, check images, and loan documents to the
investigators.

11. Plaintiffs did not consent to the disclosure of their financial records.

12. The Bank did not obtain or serve Plaintiffs with a customer notice or certification of
compliance prior to making the disclosure.

13. In addition to the unlawful disclosure, in September 2023 Plaintiffs were named as
potential witnesses in a separate federal proceeding. Defense counsel in that proceeding
served the Bank with a litigation hold letter directing the Bank to preserve all records
related to Plaintiffs' accounts.

14. Despite the litigation hold, the Bank's records management department destroyed
monthly account statements for the period October 2021 through June 2022 as part of a
routine purge in November 2023, causing irreparable prejudice to Plaintiffs' ability to
reconstruct their financial history.

15. Plaintiffs first learned of the unauthorized disclosure and the document destruction
in January 2024 when their counsel received a copy of the investigators' file through
discovery in the related proceeding.

                        CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

COUNT I
Violation of the Right to Financial Privacy Act
12 U.S.C. § 3401 et seq.

16. Plaintiffs reallege and incorporate by reference paragraphs 1 through 15.

17. Plaintiffs are "customers" of Defendant within the meaning of 12 U.S.C. § 3401(5).

18. The records disclosed were "financial records" within the meaning of 12 U.S.C.
§ 3401(2).

19. By disclosing Plaintiffs' financial records to federal investigators without complying
with the notice and challenge procedures of 12 U.S.C. §§ 3405-3407, Defendant violated
the Right to Financial Privacy Act.

20. As a direct and proximate result of this violation, Plaintiffs have suffered damages
including emotional distress, reputational harm, loss of privacy, and costs incurred in
investigating and addressing the unauthorized disclosure.

COUNT II
Negligence — Failure to Comply with Litigation Hold

21. Plaintiffs reallege and incorporate by reference paragraphs 1 through 15.

22. Defendant owed Plaintiffs a duty of care to preserve records subject to a properly
served litigation hold.

23. Defendant breached that duty by destroying account statements covered by the
litigation hold during a routine purge conducted in November 2023.

24. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's breach, Plaintiffs have suffered
damages including the loss of documentary evidence necessary to their defense in the
related federal proceeding, and costs incurred in reconstructing and authenticating
alternative records.

                        PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiffs respectfully request that this Court:

a) Enter judgment against Defendant for violation of 12 U.S.C. § 3401 et seq.;

b) Award statutory damages pursuant to 12 U.S.C. § 3417(a) of not less than $100
per day for each day of violation, up to a maximum of $10,000;

c) Award actual damages, including compensation for emotional distress, reputational
harm, and loss of privacy;

d) Award damages for Defendant's negligent destruction of records subject to the
litigation hold, in an amount to be determined at trial;

e) Award declaratory relief stating that Defendant's conduct violated the Right to
Financial Privacy Act;

f) Issue a preservation order requiring Defendant to retain all remaining records
related to Plaintiffs' accounts;

g) Award Plaintiffs their reasonable attorneys' fees and costs pursuant to
12 U.S.C. § 3417(a); and

h) Grant such other and further relief as this Court deems just and proper.
